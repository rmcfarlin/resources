# Bible Study Worksheet

## Psalms 67

*Let the Nations Praise Thee*

> God be merciful unto us, and bless us

**KJV** · Standalone study handout

---

## 1 · The Word (KJV)

**1** *God be merciful unto us, and bless us; and cause his face to shine upon us; Selah.*

**2** *That thy way may be known upon earth, thy saving health among all nations.*

**3** *Let the people praise thee, O God; let all the people praise thee.*

**4** *O let the nations be glad and sing for joy: for thou shalt judge the people righteously, and govern the nations upon earth. Selah.*

**5** *Let the people praise thee, O God; let all the people praise thee.*

**6** *Then shall the earth yield her increase; and God, even our own God, shall bless us.*

**7** *God shall bless us; and all the ends of the earth shall fear him.*

---

## 2 · Context

A brief harvest thanksgiving with strong missionary breadth, echoing the Aaronic blessing (Num 6:24–26) in its opening line and repeatedly calling all peoples and nations to praise God. Henry reads it as showing Israel's blessing was always meant to overflow toward "all the ends of the earth," not remain self-contained.

---

## 3 · Verse-by-Verse Commentary

*The notes below cover the whole chapter in one place—read with the KJV text above.*

### Verse 1-3

"God be merciful unto us, and bless us... That thy way may be known upon earth, thy saving health among all nations. Let the people praise thee, O God; let all the people praise thee." National blessing is explicitly requested for the sake of global witness, not mere comfort.

### Verse 4-7

"O let the nations be glad, and sing for joy: for thou shalt judge the people righteously... Then shall the earth yield her increase; and God, even our own God, shall bless us... and all the ends of the earth shall fear him." Harvest abundance and universal reverence are woven together as evidence of God's righteous, global governance.

---

## 4 · Meaning & Application

### Meaning

Personal and national blessing exist to make God's "way" and "saving health" known to all nations—harvest and gladness are not ends in themselves but occasions for the whole earth to fear and praise the God who governs righteously.

### Application for life today

When you experience blessing, ask how it might make God's "way" known beyond yourself, not stop with private gratitude. Pray specifically for "the nations" and "all the people" to be glad in God, widening intercession beyond personal or local concerns. Let increase in your own life become a testimony point toward God's righteous governance, not just personal comfort.
