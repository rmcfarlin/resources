# Bible Study Worksheet

## Psalms 70

*Make Haste, O God*

> Make haste, O God, to deliver me

**KJV** · Standalone study handout

---

## 1 · The Word (KJV)

**1** *Make haste, O God, to deliver me; make haste to help me, O LORD.*

**2** *Let them be ashamed and confounded that seek after my soul: let them be turned backward, and put to confusion, that desire my hurt.*

**3** *Let them be turned back for a reward of their shame that say, Aha, aha.*

**4** *Let all those that seek thee rejoice and be glad in thee: and let such as love thy salvation say continually, Let God be magnified.*

**5** *But I am poor and needy: make haste unto me, O God: thou art my help and my deliverer; O LORD, make no tarrying.*

---

## 2 · Context

A brief, urgent psalm nearly identical to the closing verses of Psalm 40 (13–17), functioning as its own short prayer for immediate rescue from those who mock the psalmist's affliction. Henry notes its repeated urgency ("make haste") twice in five verses, modeling prayer that does not pad requests with elaborate argument when danger is pressing.

---

## 3 · Verse-by-Verse Commentary

*The notes below cover the whole chapter in one place—read with the KJV text above.*

### Verse 1-3

"Make haste, O God, to deliver me... Let them be ashamed and confounded that seek after my soul... Let them be turned back for a reward of their shame that say, Aha, aha." The plea is immediate and specific: reversal of shame onto those who mock his affliction with cruel taunts.

### Verse 4-5

"Let all those that seek thee rejoice and be glad in thee... But I am poor and needy: make haste unto me, O God... O LORD, make no tarrying." The psalm balances communal joy for all seekers of God with the psalmist's own acute, personal urgency, closing exactly as it began—with an unresolved plea for speed.

---

## 4 · Meaning & Application

### Meaning

Some prayers are rightly brief and urgent rather than elaborate; naming yourself honestly as "poor and needy" and asking God not to "tarry" is itself a complete, faithful prayer, even without immediate resolution in the text.

### Application for life today

In genuine crisis, do not feel you must construct a theologically elaborate prayer before God will listen—"make haste" is enough. Let honest neediness replace performing spiritual strength you don't currently feel. Hold together, as this psalm does, urgent personal plea and genuine joy for others who seek God, rather than letting your own need eclipse all other concern.
