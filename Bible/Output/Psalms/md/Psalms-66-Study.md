## 1 · The Word (KJV)

**1** *Make a joyful noise unto God, all ye lands:*

**2** *Sing forth the honour of his name: make his praise glorious.*

**3** *Say unto God, How terrible art thou in thy works! through the greatness of thy power shall thine enemies submit themselves unto thee.*

**4** *All the earth shall worship thee, and shall sing unto thee; they shall sing to thy name. Selah.*

**5** *Come and see the works of God: he is terrible in his doing toward the children of men.*

**6** *He turned the sea into dry land: they went through the flood on foot: there did we rejoice in him.*

## 2 · Context

A communal thanksgiving psalm inviting "all ye lands" to praise God for His awesome deeds and for personally answering the psalmist's own prayer after testing. Henry notes the shift from national praise (1–12) to individual testimony and vows fulfilled (13–20), a pattern of public worship rooted in private experience.

---

## 3 · Verse-by-Verse Commentary

*The notes below cover the whole chapter in one place—read with the KJV text above.*

### Verse 1-4

"Make a joyful noise unto God, all ye lands... say unto God, How terrible art thou in thy works!... all the earth shall worship thee." Universal, exuberant praise is called for on the basis of God's fearsome, wonder-working reputation among all nations.

### Verse 5-7

"Come and see the works of God... He turned the sea into dry land: they went through the flood on foot... He ruleth by his power for ever." The Exodus is remembered as ongoing proof of present, continuing divine government over rebellious nations.

### Verse 8-12

"O bless our God, ye people... which holdeth our soul in life... thou, O God, hast proved us: thou hast tried us, as silver is tried... thou broughtest us out into a wealthy place." Testing and affliction are acknowledged honestly as God's own refining process, not meaningless suffering, ending in abundance.

### Verse 13-15

"I will go into thy house with burnt offerings: I will pay thee my vows... I will offer bullocks with goats." Private vows made during trouble are now fulfilled publicly with costly, specific worship.

### Verse 16-20

"Come and hear, all ye that fear God, and I will declare what he hath done for my soul... Verily God hath heard me... Blessed be God, which hath not turned away my prayer, nor his mercy from me." Personal testimony becomes public instruction, closing on assurance that sincere prayer is never ultimately refused.

---

## 4 · Meaning & Application

### Meaning

National memory of God's mighty acts and personal testimony of answered prayer reinforce each other; testing by fire and water is acknowledged as God's refining work, and it issues in specific, costly worship and public testimony meant to instruct others who fear God.

### Application for life today

Recount concrete "works of God"—corporately remembered and personally experienced—as a discipline of praise, not vague sentiment. When reflecting on hard seasons, consider whether God was refining you like silver, not abandoning you. Turn private vows made in trouble into specific, fulfilled acts of worship, and be willing to "declare" to others what God has done for your soul.
