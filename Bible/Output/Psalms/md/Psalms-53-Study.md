*The Fool Hath Said in His Heart*

## 1 · The Word (KJV)

**1** *The fool hath said in his heart, There is no God. Corrupt are they, and have done abominable iniquity: there is none that doeth good.*

**2** *God looked down from heaven upon the children of men, to see if there were any that did understand, that did seek God.*

**3** *Every one of them is gone back: they are altogether become filthy; there is none that doeth good, no, not one.*

**4** *Have the workers of iniquity no knowledge? who eat up my people as they eat bread: they have not called upon God.*

**5** *There were they in great fear, where no fear was: for God hath scattered the bones of him that encampeth against thee: thou hast put them to shame, because God hath despised them.*

**6** *Oh that the salvation of Israel were come out of Zion! When God bringeth back the captivity of his people, Jacob shall rejoice, and Israel shall be glad.*

---

## 2 · Context

Psalm 53 closely parallels Psalm 14, with small variations (using "Elohim" for God throughout and a different ending about the enemy). Henry notes the repetition itself is instructive—universal depravity needed restating, and God repeats truths for our slow hearts. MacArthur-style notes read "fool" as moral, not merely intellectual, folly: practical atheism that fuels corruption.

---

## 3 · Verse-by-Verse Commentary

*The notes below cover the whole chapter in one place—read with the KJV text above.*

### Verse 1-3

"The fool hath said in his heart, There is no God. Corrupt are they, and have done abominable iniquity." God looks down from heaven and finds none who understands, none who seeks Him—"they are all together become filthy." Denial of God is the root of universal corruption, not merely a private opinion.

### Verse 4-5

"Have the workers of iniquity no knowledge? who eat up my people..." they call not upon God, yet great fear will overtake them when God scatters the bones of besiegers—shame comes because God despises them. Judgment falls even on those who feel secure in oppression.

### Verse 6

"Oh that the salvation of Israel were come out of Zion! ...God bringeth back the captivity of his people." The psalm ends turning from complaint to hope for God-given deliverance and communal joy.

---

## 4 · Meaning & Application

### Meaning

Denying God is not neutral; it corrupts conduct and community. Yet the psalm does not end in cynicism—it closes longing for salvation "out of Zion," pointing every generation toward the deliverance only God can bring for a fallen people.

### Application for life today

Watch for practical atheism in yourself—living as if God does not see, even while professing belief. Let this psalm shape compassion, not superiority, toward a corrupt culture, since Scripture says none does good apart from grace. Let longing for God's salvation, not mere social critique, be your final response to evil in the world.
