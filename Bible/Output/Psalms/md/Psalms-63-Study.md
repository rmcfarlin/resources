# Bible Study Worksheet

## Psalms 63

*Early Will I Seek Thee*

> Early will I seek thee

**KJV** · Standalone study handout

---

## 1 · The Word (KJV)

**1** *O God, thou art my God; early will I seek thee: my soul thirsteth for thee, my flesh longeth for thee in a dry and thirsty land, where no water is;*

**2** *To see thy power and thy glory, so as I have seen thee in the sanctuary.*

**3** *Because thy lovingkindness is better than life, my lips shall praise thee.*

**4** *Thus will I bless thee while I live: I will lift up my hands in thy name.*

**5** *My soul shall be satisfied as with marrow and fatness; and my mouth shall praise thee with joyful lips:*

**6** *When I remember thee upon my bed, and meditate on thee in the night watches.*

**7** *Because thou hast been my help, therefore in the shadow of thy wings will I rejoice.*

**8** *My soul followeth hard after thee: thy right hand upholdeth me.*

**9** *But those that seek my soul, to destroy it, shall go into the lower parts of the earth.*

**10** *They shall fall by the sword: they shall be a portion for foxes.*

**11** *But the king shall rejoice in God; every one that sweareth by him shall glory: but the mouth of them that speak lies shall be stopped.*

---

## 2 · Context

Superscription places David "in the wilderness of Judah," likely fleeing Saul or Absalom, physically thirsty in a literal dry and barren land. Henry notes how physical thirst becomes the doorway to spiritual longing—desire for God expressed in bodily terms. MacArthur-style notes point to the psalm's structure moving from longing (1–2), to satisfaction remembered (3–5), to nighttime meditation (6–8), to confidence over enemies (9–11).

---

## 3 · Verse-by-Verse Commentary

*The notes below cover the whole chapter in one place—read with the KJV text above.*

### Verse 1-2

"O God, thou art my God; early will I seek thee: my soul thirsteth for thee... in a dry and thirsty land, where no water is." Physical deprivation in the wilderness intensifies, rather than dampens, spiritual desire—he seeks God's power and glory as once seen in the sanctuary.

### Verse 3-5

"Because thy lovingkindness is better than life, my lips shall praise thee... my soul shall be satisfied as with marrow and fatness." Even lacking literal water, he is more filled by God's lovingkindness than by any physical provision, praising with lifted hands.

### Verse 6-8

Meditation continues into sleeplessness: "When I remember thee upon my bed, I meditate on thee in the night watches... My soul followeth hard after thee: thy right hand upholdeth me." Longing does not fade in darkness but turns to sustained, pursuing meditation.

### Verse 9-11

Enemies "that seek my soul to destroy it" will fall by the sword and become "a portion for foxes," while the king will rejoice in God and every true worshiper will glory, but "the mouth of them that speak lies shall be stopped." Confidence in vindication closes the psalm.

---

## 4 · Meaning & Application

### Meaning

Genuine thirst for God is not incompatible with literal scarcity—wilderness deprivation can become the very occasion for the deepest satisfaction, praise, and pursuing meditation, because God's lovingkindness outweighs life itself.

### Application for life today

In a literal or figurative "dry and thirsty land," let physical longing point you toward greater spiritual thirst rather than only complaint. Practice remembering God specifically "upon my bed" and in "night watches" when sleep is elusive. Let satisfaction in God's lovingkindness, not changed circumstances, be the goal you actually seek "early."
