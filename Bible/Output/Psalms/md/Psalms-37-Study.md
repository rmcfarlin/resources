## 2 · Context

Psalm 37 is a wisdom acrostic tackling the age-old problem of the prosperity of the wicked, answered with a repeated refrain: fret not, trust, wait, rest, commit. It promises the meek will inherit the earth—quoted by Jesus in Matthew 5:5—and pictures the wicked withering like grass while the righteous, though they may stumble, are upheld by the Lord’s hand. Both Henry and the broader evangelical tradition treat it as a direct antidote to envy of evildoers, urging a patient, long-view faith rather than a demand for immediate justice.

## 3 · Verse-by-Verse Commentary

*The notes below cover the whole chapter in one place—read with the KJV text above.*

### Verse 1-4

Fret not because of evildoers, nor be envious of workers of iniquity, for they shall soon be cut down like grass. Instead trust in the Lord, do good, dwell in the land, feed on His faithfulness, and delight yourself in the Lord, who will give you the desires of your heart.

### Verse 5-9

Commit your way to the Lord, trust also in Him, and He shall bring it to pass. Rest in the Lord, wait patiently for Him, and cease from anger and wrath, for fretting only leads toward evil; evildoers will be cut off, but those who wait upon the Lord shall inherit the earth.

### Verse 10-15

Yet a little while and the wicked shall not be—his place will not be found. The meek inherit the earth and delight themselves in abundant peace. The wicked plots against the just and gnashes his teeth, but the Lord laughs, seeing his day of judgment coming; the sword drawn against the righteous ends up piercing the wicked man’s own heart.

### Verse 16-20

A little that the righteous have is better than the riches of many wicked, for the arms of the wicked shall be broken while the Lord upholds the righteous. The righteous are not ashamed in the evil time and are satisfied even in days of famine, while the wicked perish and consume away like smoke.

### Verse 21-26

The wicked borrows and does not repay, but the righteous is generous; those blessed by the Lord inherit the earth, and those cursed by Him are cut off. The steps of a good man are ordered by the Lord, and though he falls he is not utterly cast down, for the Lord upholds him with His hand; from youth to old age, David has never seen the righteous forsaken nor his descendants begging bread.

### Verse 27-31

Depart from evil, do good, and dwell forevermore, for the Lord loves justice and does not forsake His saints—they are preserved forever, while the seed of the wicked is cut off. The righteous shall inherit the land and dwell in it forever; their mouth speaks wisdom because the law of their God is in their heart, and their steps do not slide.

### Verse 32-36

The wicked watches the righteous, seeking to slay him, but the Lord will not leave him in the wicked’s hand nor let him be condemned when judged. Wait on the Lord and keep His way, and He will exalt you to inherit the land and see the wicked cut off; David had seen the wicked in great power, spreading like a green tree—yet he passed away and could not be found.

### Verse 37-40

Mark the perfect (blameless) man and behold the upright, for his end is peace, while transgressors are destroyed together and the end of the wicked is cut off. But the salvation of the righteous is of the Lord, their strength in time of trouble; the Lord helps and delivers them from the wicked and saves them because they trust in Him.

## 4 · Meaning & Application

### Meaning

The prosperity of the wicked is real but temporary, while the security of the righteous is often quiet and unglamorous yet permanent, upheld directly by the Lord’s own hand. Fretting, envy, and anger accomplish nothing that patient trust, doing good, and waiting on the Lord do not accomplish better and without the corrosion of bitterness.

### Application for life today

Identify the specific person or situation currently tempting you to fret or envy, and consciously replace that fretting with the psalm’s alternatives: trust, delight, commit, rest, wait. When you stumble (v. 24), remember the promise is not that the righteous never fall but that the Lord upholds them when they do. Practice “mark the perfect man” (v. 37) by looking for quiet, faithful examples rather than only loud, prosperous ones.
