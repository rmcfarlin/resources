# Bible Study Worksheet

## Psalms 3

*A Morning Shield*

> Lord, how are they increased that trouble me!

**KJV** · Standalone study handout

---

## 1 · The Word (KJV)

**1** *LORD, how are they increased that trouble me! many are they that rise up against me.*

**2** *Many there be which say of my soul, There is no help for him in God. Selah.*

**3** *But thou, O LORD, art a shield for me; my glory, and the lifter up of mine head.*

**4** *I cried unto the LORD with my voice, and he heard me out of his holy hill. Selah.*

**5** *I laid me down and slept; I awaked; for the LORD sustained me.*

**6** *I will not be afraid of ten thousands of people, that have set themselves against me round about.*

**7** *Arise, O LORD; save me, O my God: for thou hast smitten all mine enemies upon the cheek bone; thou hast broken the teeth of the ungodly.*

**8** *Salvation belongeth unto the LORD: thy blessing is upon thy people. Selah.*

---

## 2 · Context

Superscription (in the Hebrew Bible tradition): a psalm of David when he fled from Absalom. Psalm 3 is a personal lament that becomes confidence—often called a morning psalm because of verse 5 (sleep and waking). Henry notes the psalmist’s crisis is multiplied enemies and taunts that God will not help; faith answers with God as shield and sustainer. It follows the royal confidence of Psalm 2 with a king under siege.

---

## 3 · Verse-by-Verse Commentary

*The notes below cover the whole chapter in one place—read with the KJV text above.*

### Verse 1-2

Complaint: enemies increase; many rise up; mockers say there is no help in God. The pain is social and spiritual—isolation plus blasphemous taunt. Selah invites a pause under the weight of the crisis.

### Verse 3-4

Turning point: “But thou, O Lord, art a shield for me; my glory, and the lifter up of mine head.” Prayer is vocal and answered from God’s holy hill. Protection, dignity, and restoration of courage meet in one confession.

### Verse 5-6

Evidence of trust: he sleeps and wakes because the Lord sustains him. Fear of “ten thousands” is refused—not by denial of danger, but by confidence in God’s sustaining presence.

### Verse 7-8

Petition and praise: arise, save; God has already struck the ungodly’s power (“broken the teeth”). Salvation belongs to the Lord; blessing rests on His people. Personal deliverance expands to corporate hope.

---

## 4 · Meaning & Application

### Meaning

When opposition multiplies and voices deny God’s help, faith looks away from the count of enemies to the character of God—shield, glory, head-lifter, sustainer. Sleep becomes a theological act. Salvation is the Lord’s gift, not self-rescue.

### Application for life today

Anxiety often multiplies at night and in isolation. Practice Psalm 3’s pattern: name the trouble honestly, then preach God as shield to your own soul. Ask where mockery (“God won’t help”) has entered your thinking. Entrust sleep and waking to the Sustainer; refuse to let enemy-counts set your fear level.
