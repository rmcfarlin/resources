# Source tradition for Bible study worksheets

Worksheets use the **King James Version** (public domain) for the Scripture block.

Context, meaning, and application are **original summaries** informed by classic and widely used evangelical exposition—not wholesale copies of proprietary study notes.

## Primary public-domain commentary

- **Matthew Henry**, *Commentary on the Whole Bible* (public domain) — structure, pastoral force, holiness/happiness contrasts
- **Charles Spurgeon**, *The Treasury of David* (Psalms; public domain) — when enriching psalm meditations

## Study-Bible / expository tradition (themes, paraphrased)

Summarize in your own words; do **not** paste copyrighted study-note prose:

- John MacArthur Study Bible / MacArthur expositions (two ways; delight in the law; planted vs. chaff; Lord knows the righteous)
- Standard evangelical study Bible consensus (ESV Study Bible, NIV Study Bible type notes) on genre, structure, and key cross-references
- Cross-references commonly cited: Jer 17:5–8; Matt 7:13–14, 24–27; Josh 1:8; Deut 6:6–9

## Attribution line (footer)

Use a short form such as:

> Scripture: KJV (public domain). Study notes summarize themes from Matthew Henry and the broader evangelical study-Bible tradition (including MacArthur-style exposition), rewritten for this handout.

## Integrity

- Never invent verse text
- Never present paraphrased study notes as direct quotes from MacArthur or a study Bible
- Prefer short, teachable sections suitable for a one-page handout
