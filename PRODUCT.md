# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Solo adults training at home, usually right before or after a mat session. They want a clear printed plan they can mark up with a pen—no phone required mid-set. Primary audience is one trainee following an 8-week push-up protocol; a future digital app may serve the same person later.

## Product Purpose

A printable Fallout-themed push-up tracking sheet that guides someone from their current max to **100 consecutive push-ups in 8 weeks**. Success is finishing the protocol with a logged set of 100 in a row, and having every session’s planned vs actual reps recorded so progress is visible on paper.

## Positioning

Vault-Tec field strength clearance: a wasteland training protocol that turns ordinary push-ups into a mission with a hard target—100 consecutive.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: Print the protocol and complete Day 0 baseline, then follow Week 1 Day A.
- Secondary fallback: Read the “Form & Safety” strip and adjust to an easier progression tier if baseline is low.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: **Eight weeks. One hundred in a row. Clearance granted.**
- Belief ladder: (1) 100 consecutive is achievable with a plan, (2) this protocol’s numbers are sane, (3) logging planned vs actual keeps you honest, (4) Fallout framing makes sticking with it fun.
- Proof on hand: User-supplied Fallout mood reference (power armor / Capital Wasteland still) at `.cursor/projects/.../assets/` for tone; no testimonials yet.

## Brand Personality

Wry, mission-brief, Vault-Tec optimistic-apocalypse. Three words: **gritty, procedural, hopeful**. Emotion: determined resolve with a smirk—not parody spam, not grimdark despair.

## Anti-references

- Dark Pip-Boy CRT / green-on-black HUD (reserved for a future app, not for print)
- Generic neon “gamer” fitness templates
- Cute sticker-bomb Vault Boy collage that buries the workout numbers
- Dense TinyText bootcamp PDFs that waste ink and fail at arm’s length

## Design Principles

1. **Mission over decoration** — Every graphic earns its place by making the week’s work clearer.
2. **Ink-honest print** — Light paper ground, high-contrast type; readable under home lighting after a set.
3. **Honest logging** — Planned vs actual is the ritual; the sheet never shames a miss, it records it.
4. **Wasteland with restraint** — Fallout cues (Vault-Tec language, stamps, olive/rust accents) without drowning the grid.
5. **As many pages as clarity needs** — Prefer an extra page over cramping eight weeks onto one sheet.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast as a floor for any on-screen preview; print must stay high-contrast (dark ink on light paper). Large enough type for reading while standing over a mat (~11–12pt body minimum on paper). No information carried by color alone. Respect reduced-motion if a later web preview adds flourishes.
